About

I build systems that learn — not systems that follow rules.

My instinct is to replace static heuristics with trainable policies. A city's traffic grid running on cooperative RL agents. A climate model that captures 40 years of ENSO dynamics at multiple temporal scales. A data intelligence assistant that turns noisy city complaints into actionable insight. The pattern is the same: define the environment, shape the objective, train until emergent behaviour beats the baseline.

I work at the intersection of simulation, deep learning, agentic orchestration, and real-world deployment — drawn to problems where failure has measurable cost: congestion, delayed diagnosis, unintelligible civic data.

HYDAI — Autonomous Data Intelligence Assistant
Groq LLaMA · LangGraph · ChromaDB · React · N8N · Pandas · FastAPI
Problem
Urban complaint data is deeply noisy — missing fields, inconsistent spellings, duplicates, and free-text that resists structured analysis. Without a robust pipeline, pattern detection and civic resource allocation are impossible.
Architecture
End-to-end AI pipeline: Pandas fuzzy deduplication cleans raw data, Groq LLaMA structures free-text into schema, and a LangGraph multi-agent graph routes queries through a RAG layer (ChromaDB). Automated via N8N. Frontend: React dashboard with a Hyderabad complaint map and an LLM chat interface.
Why It Matters
Civic AI at scale — turning unstructured city noise into queryable intelligence. The ingest → clean → embed → agent-route pattern applies to enterprise data ops, legal document intelligence, and healthcare complaint triage.

Problem
Fixed-timer signals operate on rigid cycles with zero perception of real-time conditions — producing cascading congestion and excess emissions even on low-load roads. No static heuristic coordinates across all junction states simultaneously.
Architecture
Multi-agent PPO across a 2×2 grid of 4 cooperative junctions in SUMO + TraCI. Agents observe local queue lengths and neighbour phase states, trained with cooperative reward shaping that penalises system-wide wait time. Includes real-time Pygame visualiser and auto-generated HTML analytics dashboard.
Why It Matters
Urban traffic control is a canonical multi-agent coordination problem. Cooperative reward shaping and neighbour-aware policy design here directly generalise to logistics routing, drone swarm coordination, and industrial process control.

El Niño / La Niña Climate Forecaster
LSTM · RNN · Hybrid Temporal Modeling · TensorFlow
Problem
ENSO events drive global weather anomalies affecting agriculture, water supply, and disaster risk across continents. Predicting them requires capturing non-linear temporal dependencies spanning decades — a task where statistical baselines structurally fail.
Architecture
Hybrid LSTM + RNN forecasting pipeline processing climate observations at multiple temporal resolutions. LSTM layers capture long-range memory while RNN layers encode short-range seasonal dynamics — producing a multi-scale representation that outperforms single-architecture baselines. Awarded Best Paper, 2nd International IEEE Conference.
Why It Matters
Accurate ENSO forecasting 6–12 months ahead has measurable economic impact: crop yield planning, insurance pricing, emergency pre-positioning. The hybrid temporal architecture applies to any long-horizon time series with multi-frequency structure.
